Ashwagandha KSM-66®
300mg · Full-spectrum root
World's most clinically studied ashwagandha extract. Reduces cortisol by 27.9% in double-blind, placebo-controlled trials. Improves sleep onset and total sleep time. KSM-66® maintains the full root profile including withanolides — not a generic powder.
Chandrasekhar et al. (2012) — Indian Journal of Psychological Medicine
Magnesium Glycinate
200mg elemental · Chelated
4× more bioavailable than cheap Magnesium Oxide. Essential for GABA-A receptor activation — the brain's primary relaxation pathway. Directly involved in tryptophan-to-melatonin conversion. Magnesium deficiency is strongly associated with insomnia in multiple population studies.
Abbasi et al. (2012) — Journal of Research in Medical Sciences
L-Theanine (Suntheanine®)
200mg · Patented pure-isomer form
Promotes alpha brain wave activity — the relaxed-but-alert state of meditation. Synergises powerfully with Magnesium Glycinate. You fall asleep naturally, not knocked out. No next-day sedation. Suntheanine® is the patented form used in clinical research — not generic L-Theanine.
Kimura et al. (2007) — Biological Psychology
Montmorency Cherry
500mg · Standardised extract
One of the only natural food sources of melatonin. Significantly increases sleep duration and ORAC antioxidant capacity. Our standardised extract provides consistent natural melatonin precursors plus anti-inflammatory polyphenols that reduce the inflammation associated with poor sleep.
Pigeon et al. (2010) — Journal of Medicinal Food
Lemon Balm + Passionflower
300mg + 150mg
Both act on GABA pathways to promote natural relaxation without dependency risk. Passionflower showed effects comparable to low-dose benzodiazepines in clinical trials — without the side effects. The final layer of Bedtime Bliss's multi-pathway sleep system.
Kennedy et al. (2004) — Psychosomatic Medicine

Lactobacillus Acidophilus
10 Billion CFU · Microencapsulated
Microencapsulated — survives stomach acid and reaches the large intestine alive. 10B CFU is the clinically meaningful threshold. Our L. Acidophilus is the most studied probiotic strain in human gut health research.
Ringel et al. (2012) — Gastroenterology Clinics of North America
Psyllium Husk
500mg · 70% soluble fibre
Gold-standard prebiotic fibre. Forms a protective gel in the gut that coats the intestinal lining, feeds beneficial bacteria, and promotes healthy transit time. The structural backbone of any credible gut health formula.
Giannini et al. (2006) — American Journal of Gastroenterology
Artichoke Leaf Extract
400mg · 5:1 · 5% cynarin
Cynarin stimulates bile production by the liver and release from the gallbladder. Adequate bile is essential for fat digestion — its absence causes post-meal bloating. Standardised to 5% cynarin for consistent potency.
Holtmann et al. (2003) — Alimentary Pharmacology & Therapeutics
Slippery Elm Bark
300mg · Inner bark powder
Creates a protective mucilage layer when it contacts water in the gut — soothing and repairing the intestinal lining. Directly addresses gut permeability and reduces IBS-related inflammation.
Watts et al. (2019) — Journal of Alternative Medicine
Milk Thistle (Silymarin)
200mg · 80% silymarin
Hepatoprotective — liver-protecting. Silymarin demonstrated liver cell regeneration in 30+ clinical studies. A healthy liver directly improves digestion and toxin processing. Standardised to 80% silymarin — highest clinically justified concentration.
Saller et al. (2001) — Drugs
Ginger Root Extract
250mg · 5% gingerols
Gingerols accelerate gastric emptying — slow gastric emptying is the primary cause of post-meal bloating. Also reduces pro-inflammatory prostaglandins responsible for gut pain and cramping.
Ernst & Pittler (2000) — British Journal of Anaesthesia
